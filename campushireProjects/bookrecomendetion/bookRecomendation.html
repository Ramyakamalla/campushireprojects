SCIENCE FICTION

Dune

By Frank Herbert

An epic science fiction novel set in a distant future amidst a sprawling intersteller empire.
Set on the desert planet Arrakis, Dune is the story of the boy Paul Atreides,
their to a noble family tasked with ruling an inhospitable world
where the only thing of value is the “spice” melange, a drug capable of extending life and enhancing consciousness.

Neuromancer

By William Gibson

A cyberpunk masterpiece that explores the intersection of technology and humanity.
Gibson coined the term "cyberspace" for "widespread, interconnected digital technology"
in his short story "Burning Chrome" (1982), and later popularized the concept in his acclaimed debut novel Neuromancer (1980).
